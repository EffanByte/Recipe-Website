Rice with Chicken Ginger

This savory dish is made by cooking the white rice separately from the chicken ginger , which is very different from the traditional method of cooking them all in the same pot. The chicken gravy is cooked separately and then added to white rice once they're boiled. The plainness of the rice makes it very appealing to eat with the strong and tomato-ish taste of the chicken ginger.

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups of Rice
  • 1 Tomato
  • 2 stems of ginger
  • One Onion
  • Chilli powder
  • One clove of Spearmint
  • 250 g of Chicken breasts
  • Salt and Pepper

Steps

  1. Take the two cups of rice and soak them in water for at least an hour
  2. Take the chicken breasts and chop them up into small pieces, preferably about 1 inch tall and wide.
  3. Cut up the tomato into small slices and toss it into a searing hot pan that's been oiled on medium heat
  4. After two minutes, add in the onions and the chilli masala to the pan. At this point, the tomato should be becoming a paste.
  5. Add About two table spoons of salt to the pan, and mix.
  6. Once everything has dissolved down, add the chicken to the mixture and stir it. Wait 10 minutes for the chicken to cook, and take it out.
  7. As for the rice, add your two cups of rice to the pressure cooker and let them cook for about 30 minutes. Make sure to add a tablespoon of salt in between.
  8. Let the rice sit in the pressure cooker for at least 10 minutes before opening the lid.
  9. Once the rice has been boiled, take it out on a plate, and carefully pour the chicken ginger on top of the rice
